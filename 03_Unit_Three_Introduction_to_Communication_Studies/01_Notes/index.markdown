# Notes - Unit 3

# Section 1: Foundations of Communication

## Meaning of Communication

Communication is the process of sharing information, ideas, thoughts, and feelings from one person to another. It involves a sender, a message, a channel, and a receiver. Communication can be intentional or unintentional, and it happens in everyday life.

Communication helps people understand each other. It allows individuals and groups to interact, cooperate, and live together in society.

---

## Modalities of Communication

Modalities of communication are the different ways communication happens.

### Verbal Communication

Verbal communication is communication through words. It includes:

- **Spoken communication** – face-to-face talk, phone calls, meetings, speeches
- **Written communication** – letters, emails, messages, books, reports

Verbal communication helps people share clear ideas, instructions, and information.

### Non-Verbal Communication

Non-verbal communication is communication without words. It includes:

- Body language
- Facial expressions
- Gestures
- Posture
- Eye contact
- Tone of voice
- Feelings and emotions
- Personal space
- Appearance

Non-verbal communication supports verbal communication and can sometimes send stronger messages than words.

---

## Why Communication Is Central to Human Interaction and Survival

Communication is important to human life and survival because it helps people:

- Build and maintain relationships
- Share culture and traditions (cultural continuity)
- Solve problems together
- Learn new ideas and skills
- Support personal and social growth
- Ensure safety and survival
- Express needs and emotions
- Coordinate activities and work

Without communication, society cannot function well.

---

## Importance of Communication

Communication plays a key role in society. Its importance includes:

- Influences people and supports decision making
- Brings change in attitudes and behavior
- Promotes organizational success through teamwork
- Enables cultural exchange
- Facilitates learning and education
- Helps in transfer of knowledge and skills
- Preserves oral traditions and cultural beliefs
- Builds relationships
- Fosters trust and strong connections
- Reduces conflict and misunderstanding
- Improves leadership and management

---

## Forms of Communication

Communication takes different forms depending on the situation.

### Intrapersonal Communication

Intrapersonal communication is communication within oneself. It includes thinking, self-talk, and reflection. It helps a person understand themselves and make decisions.

### Interpersonal Communication

Interpersonal communication is communication between two people. It helps in building relationships.

### Small Group Communication

Small group communication involves a few people, such as in meetings or group discussions.

### Organizational Communication

Organizational communication happens within an organization between workers, leaders, and departments.

### Intercultural Communication

Intercultural communication occurs between people from different cultures. It promotes understanding and respect.

### Public Communication (Public Speaking)

Public communication involves one person speaking to many people, such as during speeches or lectures.

### Mass Communication

Mass communication sends messages to large audiences through media like radio, television, newspapers, and the internet.

# Section 2: Listening and Effective Communication

## Listening and Hearing: A Comparison

### Hearing

Hearing is the passive process of receiving sound through the ears.

- It is natural and automatic
- It happens without effort
- It occurs only when the auditory system is working
- It does not require concentration

### Listening

Listening is the active process of paying attention to sounds and making meaning from them.

- It is voluntary and intentional
- It requires mental focus
- It involves understanding and interpreting messages
- It needs emotional engagement

### Listening vs Hearing (Summary)

- **Nature**: Hearing is passive; listening is active
- **Process**: Hearing receives sound; listening interprets meaning
- **Attention**: Hearing needs no focus; listening needs attention
- **Purpose**: Hearing has no goal; listening aims to understand
- **Outcome**: Hearing produces sound awareness; listening produces understanding

---

## Key Features of Effective Verbal Communication

Effective verbal communication depends on:

- Clarity of message
- Appropriate vocabulary
- Logical structure
- Suitable tone
- Accuracy
- Feedback

### Limitations of Verbal Communication

- Misinterpretation due to unclear or ambiguous words
- Requires language ability for both sender and receiver
- Written communication lacks immediate feedback
- Emotional meaning may be lost
- Noise and distractions may interfere

---

## Non-Verbal Communication (Overview)

Non-verbal communication is the transfer of information without spoken or written words. It often works together with verbal communication.

### Functions of Non-Verbal Communication

- Supports spoken messages
- Expresses emotions
- Shows attitudes and intentions
- Regulates interaction

### Characteristics of Non-Verbal Communication

- Mostly unconscious
- Culture-based
- Continuous
- Powerful in meaning

### Limitations of Non-Verbal Communication

- Can be misunderstood across cultures
- Meanings are not always clear
- Hard to control

### Forms of Non-Verbal Communication

- Facial expressions
- Gestures
- Posture
- Eye contact
- Body movement
- Appearance

---

## Paralinguistic Features (Paralanguage)

Paralanguage refers to vocal qualities that accompany speech but are not words.

### Components of Paralanguage

- Tone of voice
- Pitch
- Rate of speech
- Volume
- Pauses
- Stress and emphasis

These features influence how messages are understood.

# Section 3: Introduction to Mass Communication Theories

## Magic Bullet / Hypodermic Needle Theory

This theory suggests that media messages are directly received and accepted by audiences without resistance.

## Two-Step Flow Theory

This theory explains that media effects are indirect. Messages first reach opinion leaders, who then influence others.

## Uses and Gratifications Theory

This theory views audiences as active users who choose media to satisfy needs such as information, entertainment, and social interaction.

## Agenda-Setting Theory

This theory states that media influences what people think about by highlighting certain issues.

## Spiral of Silence Theory

This theory explains that people may stay silent when they feel their opinions are unpopular.

---

## Roles of Mass Media in Society

- Surveillance (information and news)
- Education
- Cultural transmission
- Entertainment
- Social linkage
- Economic development
- Public awareness

## 1. Public Speaking

Public speaking is the act of speaking to an audience to inform, persuade, or entertain. It is audience-centered and requires clear planning and delivery.

### Elements of Effective Public Speaking

- **Content**: Clear ideas, accurate information, and well-researched points
- **Delivery / Presentation**: Voice, posture, eye contact, gestures, and confidence
- **Audience**: Understanding audience needs, interests, and background

Effective public speaking focuses on the audience, not the speaker.

---

### Types of Public Speaking

- **Informative speaking** – to educate or explain
- **Persuasive speaking** – to influence opinions or actions
- **Ceremonial speaking** – speeches for special occasions
- **Demonstrative speaking** – showing how something is done

---

### Barriers to Effective Public Speaking

- Educational misconceptions
- Monotony in voice or delivery
- Use of jargon
- Disorganized content
- Poor preparation
- Fear and anxiety

---

### Overcoming Public Speaking Anxiety

- Practice regularly
- Use breathing exercises
- Prepare well
- Focus on the message, not fear
- Gain experience through speaking opportunities

---

### Speech Preparation Framework

1. Choose a topic
2. Define the purpose
3. Know the audience
4. Research the content
5. Organize and structure ideas
6. Plan delivery
7. Prepare visual aids
8. Practice and manage anxiety

---

### Common Barriers to Effective Communication in Speeches

- Nervousness
- Poor time management
- Use of fillers (e.g., "uh", "um")
- Lack of clarity
- Weak conclusion
